Born

Since I was a child, my interest verted in technical and scientific argument, asking myself and who knows about, every time “How does this thing works?”. I inherited mechanical love from my mother’s family, in particular my grandad was an entrepreneur and a mechanical expert who explained to me the beauty and elegance of the mechanical process. On the other hand, my uncle is an electrical engineer, so he introduced me to the IT and electronic world.

RC race

As an Italian I consider myself a heart fan of cars, so much that on my 10th I started to compete in some RC race, facing the hardness, truth, pressure and stress in a race weekend. I competed until I passed 20 in some categories such as: 1:10 nitro and 1:10 electric, in national championships such as UISP, XRS and regional with the TG1 Hobby club. Because of RC my interest in technical things became so strong that lead me to go into detail about electronic and mechanical component.

Growth

Through this period, I chose to enroll in a mechanical technical high school (I.I.S. Alessandro Volta). That was a terrific experience that gave me the basic theoretical fundamentals of the mechanical design, IT and electrical, through laboratories experience in the mechanic shop (lathe, drill, welding machine and other) and in the IT laboratories for the mechanical drawing (SolidWorks, AutoCAD, Solid EDGE, and other) and for the fundamentals of the language program and the basic electronic to run an Arduino. Even if IT wasn’t my school course specialization, I early loved computers, and I was having fun understanding how it works and how to trouble shoot or how to change OS in a machine, so I explored at first the Windows OS, then the macOS and in the end the Linux OS.

Present

Since I was a kid my goal was to become an engineer, so I enrolled at first to a three-year degree in Mechanic and then to a Master of Science in Mechanic and Industrial Automation, both in the University of Parma. This choice was natural because of my interest in IT and robotics, so MS in Mechanic and Industrial Automation waas the best choise because is giving to me notion about how to make a robot, how to control it and what are the best components to adopt for each case scenario.
